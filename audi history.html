The Beginnings of Audi

Audi was founded by August Horch in 1909. The company’s name comes from the Latin translation of Horch’s surname, which means "listen." In 1932, four companies—Audi, DKW, Horch, and Wanderer—merged to form Auto Union AG. This merger is symbolized by the iconic four rings logo that represents the heritage of these brands.

Known for technological innovation and exceptional craftsmanship, Audi introduced front-wheel drive vehicles as early as the 1930s, setting a trend in automotive design. During the 1980s, Audi revolutionized rally racing with the Audi Quattro, a groundbreaking all-wheel-drive system.

About the Founders

August Horch, a German engineer and automobile pioneer, established Horch & Cie. Motorwagenwerke in 1899. However, due to differences with the company's board, he left and founded his new company in 1909. Unable to use the "Horch" brand name legally, he named the new company "Audi," using the Latin word for "listen."

In 1932, the merger of Audi, DKW, Horch, and Wanderer marked the beginning of the Auto Union era, setting the stage for one of the most recognized car manufacturers today.

Milestones in Audi History

  • 1932: Formation of Auto Union AG and introduction of the four rings logo.
  • 1980: Introduction of Audi Quattro, revolutionizing motorsports with all-wheel drive.
  • 1994: Debut of the Audi A8, a luxury sedan showcasing Audi's lightweight aluminum space frame.
  • 2006: Launch of the Audi R8, a mid-engine supercar defining Audi's performance ambitions.
  • 2020: Commitment to electrification with the Audi e-tron, entering the age of sustainable mobility.
